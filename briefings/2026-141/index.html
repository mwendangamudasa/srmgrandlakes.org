The World Health Organization has formally declared the Ebola outbreak spanning the DRC and Uganda a Public Health Emergency of International Concern — the highest alert level under international public health law. Cross-border transmission risk in the Great Lakes corridor remains uncontained.
SRM Alert

All personnel and implementing partners active in eastern DRC and Uganda must immediately review health protocols, medical evacuation plans, and PPE requirements. A dedicated SRM Great Lakes Health Security Advisory is forthcoming.

Geopolitical · U.S.–Cuba
21 May 2026
Indictment of Raúl Castro
Diplomatic escalation · Americas security environment
24 February 1996
Cuban military aircraft shot down two civilian planes by Brothers to the Rescue. Four U.S. citizens killed. Cuba denies criminal liability.
1996 – 2024
Incident remains a persistent flashpoint across multiple U.S. administrations. No individual accountability established. Cuba declines extradition.
21 May 2026 — Today
U.S. federal grand jury formally indicts former Cuban President Raúl Castro. Most significant legal escalation in decades. Bilateral frameworks near total collapse.

Operational Implication: Organizations operating under U.S. federal funding or reliant on U.S.–Cuba travel and trade frameworks should anticipate accelerating restrictions. Diplomatic channels likely suspended near-term. Monitor State Department advisories closely.
